Mellanni product pages list the same core care instructions across Iconic microfiber sheet sets: machine wash cold, tumble dry low, do not bleach, and do not iron. The supplied care PDF applies to microfiber bedding only and adds cycle, detergent, lint, and heat guidance. The Mellanni guide adds practical care guidance for lint control, first wash, fabric softener, and storage.

<Info>
  This page covers Mellanni microfiber sheets. Do not reuse the supplied PDF guidance for Egyptian cotton or other fabric families unless their product label says the same thing.
</Info>

## Standard care

<Steps>
  <Step title="Wash with cold water">
    Use cold water on a permanent press or gentle cycle. Wash with similar colors.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Keep microfiber away from lint-heavy fabrics">
    Wash microfiber sheets separately or with similar smooth fabrics. Do not mix them with terry towels, cotton sheets, fleece, denim, or rough items that shed lint or increase abrasion.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use a gentle detergent">
    Use mild detergent. Avoid bleach, fabric softener, and dryer sheets.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dry on low heat">
    Tumble dry low. Remove promptly when dry to reduce wrinkles.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fold or remake the bed">
    Smooth the fitted sheet by hand while placing it on the mattress.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to avoid

| Avoid | Reason |
| --- | --- |
| Bleach | Product pages say not to bleach |
| Ironing | Product pages say not to iron |
| High heat drying | Low heat is the listed drying instruction; the supplied care PDF says high heat can melt fibers and make them feel rough |
| Dry cleaning | Product and FAQ care guidance says not to dry clean |
| Fabric softener or dryer sheets | The supplied care PDF says fabric softener can pill fibers; the Mellanni guide says softener and dryer sheets can coat microfiber and reduce moisture-wicking performance |
| Overloading washer or dryer | Crowding can increase wrinkling and uneven drying |

<Tip>
  For fewer wrinkles, move sheets from washer to dryer soon after the cycle ends, dry on low, and remove when dry instead of leaving them in a hot dryer drum.
</Tip>

## Stains and repeated washing

The product pages describe the sheets as stain, fade, shrink, pill, and wrinkle resistant. Treat stains quickly and avoid high heat until the stain is removed, because heat can set some stains into fabric.

## Pilling and texture

Pilling can be affected by friction, wash load, detergent amount, and dryer heat. To reduce abrasion:

- Wash sheets with bedding or other soft textiles.
- Avoid washing with terry towels, cotton sheets, rough garments, hook-and-loop closures, or heavy zippers.
- Do not overload the washer.
- Use low heat in the dryer.
- Avoid fabric softener and dryer sheets.

## Care FAQ from the product question section

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I machine wash the sheets?">
    Yes. Use cold water and a permanent press or gentle cycle.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I tumble dry them?">
    Yes. Tumble dry low and remove the sheets promptly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Should I use fabric softener?">
    No. The supplied care PDF says fabric softener can pill microfiber fibers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I iron or dry clean them?">
    No. Do not iron or dry clean microfiber sheets. The supplied care PDF says high heat can melt fibers and make them rough.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will they shrink, wrinkle, or fade?">
    Mellanni product pages describe the sheets as shrink resistant, wrinkle resistant, and fade resistant. Follow the cold-wash and low-heat drying guidance to protect those qualities.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Storage

Store sheets dry and folded. If storing a full set, place the fitted sheet and flat sheet inside one pillowcase so the set stays together.

The Mellanni guide also recommends storing clean, fully dry sheets in a cool, dry place and using the included storage bag when available.
